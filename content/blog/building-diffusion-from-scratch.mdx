I rebuilt a latent diffusion model from the paper up five times over eight months. Each
attempt taught me something the last one couldn't, and the final version only trained
stably once I stopped blaming the architecture and started blaming my data pipeline.

## The thing nobody tells you

Most diffusion tutorials hand you a clean dataset and a working VAE. The hard part isn't
the rectified-flow objective or the MMDiT blocks — it's everything around them:

- Latents that aren't normalized the way you think they are
- A sampler that silently disagrees with your training schedule
- Augmentations that look fine but quietly shift the distribution

## Where it finally clicked

The fifth iteration trains stably and samples 256px images from a checkpoint I trained
myself. The difference wasn't a smarter model — it was rebuilding the data pipeline so the
latents actually matched what the model expected at sample time.

> If your loss looks fine but your samples are garbage, look at your data before your model.
